## What can you do with the GRI Risk Viewer?

The aim of this website is to collect and present open, climate-related risk
data. This can help to build understanding of the state of climate risks,
particularly to infrastructure systems (including transport, energy and water).

We do this transparently, citing [sources](/data), and making [data
extracts](/downloads) available to download, alongside the interactive maps
that make up the main content of the site.

For example, as in the map above, several river and coastal flooding datasets
are available to explore, so across different return periods and climate
scenarios, we can understand the intensity and location of flooding, at least as
far as is represented by the best-available open data with global coverage.

We also map the population, infrastructure and other assets that are exposed to a
variety of hazards.

And then, as a first step of analysis, it is possible to overlay hazards
and assets (also called exposure datasets, because these are the things exposed
to hazards), to see where risks may be concentrated.

In the map below, a red trunk road and purple primary road are both crossing a
dark blue area which is showing more than five metres of flooding in a
one-in-one-hundred year return period flood (that is, this flood model output
finds that there is a one percent chance of flood depths exceeding five metres
in any given year, in the current climate).

Hover over the map to see flood depths at different locations. Use the +/-
buttons to zoom, and click and drag to pan around.

## Where could we go next?

The collection of hazard data is one place to start exploring further. The "site
inspection tool" is enabled by clicking on the mouse-arrow-in-circles icon in
the top-left corner of the map, just under the search icon and layers control.
If you then click anywhere on the map, a sidebar will appear on the right with
data on all of the hazard layers that are available for [that location](/view/hazard?y=51.40304&x=-0.34733&z=12&sections=%7B"hazards"%3A%7B"fluvial"%3A%7B"jrc"%3Atrue%7D%7D%7D&site="51.405172%2C-0.348455").
